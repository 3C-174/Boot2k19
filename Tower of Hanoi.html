A Glimpse at the Puzzle

The puzzle was invented back in 19th century.It was played by young priests in Hindu Temples to furnish their mental discipline and focus.They practiced the puzzle with 50 disks arranged on one of the three plates.Each disk is placed on slightly larger disk.The aim is to re-create the tower formed to a different plate by moving disks one disk at a time.In addition to this condition,a larger disk cannot be placed on smaller disk. As we increase the number of disks we observe patterns which helps us decipher this conventional and celebrated puzzle.One may utilise the concept of Recursive Functions and Stacks to decode the puzzle.

Instructions

1.Only one disk can be moved at a time.

2.Each move consists of taking the upper disk from one of the stacks and placing on top of another stack.In other words,a disk can only be moved if it is the uppermost disk on a stack.

3.No larger disk maybe placed on top of a smaller disk.
